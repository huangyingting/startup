Manufacturing cybersecurity protects production systems, automation equipment, and industrial control systems from cyber threats that could disrupt operations, steal intellectual property, or deploy ransomware.

Manufacturing operations have low tolerance for downtime, making them prime ransomware targets. Additionally, manufacturers hold valuable intellectual property and supply chain data attractive to cyber adversaries.

Successful attacks can halt production lines, disrupt supply chains, steal trade secrets, and cause significant financial losses. Even brief disruptions can have cascading effects across operations.

Ransomware poses the greatest risk, with 1,171 manufacturing organizations attacked in 2024. Other risks include IP theft, supply chain attacks, and exploitation of internet-exposed industrial devices.
