OVERVIEW

Source: Secondary Research, Interviews with Experts, MarketsandMarkets Analysis

The cloud security market is projected to reach USD 59.34 billion by 2031 from USD 34.37 billion in 2026, at a CAGR of 11.5%. Multi-cloud and hybrid cloud adoption trends are driving the emergence of cloud security solutions that provide seamless visibility, compliance, and threat management across diverse infrastructure types. In addition, DevSecOps and cloud-native application development trends have made it essential for organizations to integrate security into their application development processes. The combined influence of these trends has increased demand for scalable cloud security solutions that protect dynamic workloads and enable faster application deployments.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

BY REGION

North America is expected to account for the largest share of 32.2% of the cloud security market in 2026.

BY OFFERING

By offering, the platform/solutions segment is expected to dominate the market by capturing the largest market share in 2026.

BY TYPE

By type, the cloud-native application protection platform (CNAPP) segment is expected to register the highest CAGR of 14.6% during the forecast period.

BY DEPLOYMENT MODE

By deployment mode, hybrid cloud will be the fastest-growing segment from 2026 to 2031.

BY ORGANIZATION SIZE

By organization size, the large enterprises segment is expected to hold the largest market share in 2026.

BY VERTICAL

By vertical, the healthcare and life sciences segment is likely to record the highest CAGR of 15.1% during the forecast period.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE - KEY PLAYERS

Microsoft, AWS, and Palo Alto Networks are leading players in the cloud security market, offering integrated, AI-driven platforms spanning CNAPP, identity security, and threat detection, with strong multi-cloud capabilities, deep enterprise integration, and end-to-end protection across applications, workloads, and infrastructure.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE - STARTUPS/SMEs

Orca Security, Upwind Security, and Menlo Security are emerging innovators providing cloud-native security platforms focused on agentless scanning, runtime visibility, and AI-driven risk prioritization, enabling real-time threat detection and streamlined remediation across dynamic multi-cloud environments.

Organizations are recognizing the importance of real-time threat detection and automated response mechanisms that enable them to react promptly to threats within dynamic cloud environments. Consequently, the utilization of artificial intelligence-based security solutions that can scan large amounts of data for potential risks is on the rise.

The cloud security industry is evolving from the conventional perimeter security model to a new security ecosystem that incorporates platforms and AI-enabled models tailored to the needs of the dynamic cloud environment. This figure shows that the revenue mix in the cloud security industry is evolving due to an increase in demand for CNAPP, CASB, and zero trust models. It also shows that specific needs within the BFSI, government, and healthcare sectors are driving security investments and resulting in desired outcomes.

Multi-cloud and hybrid environments have become popular to prevent vendor lock-in and improve security and resiliency, driving high demand for security platforms that integrate multiple functions. According to Microsoft, in February 2025, it is important to achieve cross-cloud visibility with security tools such as Defender and Sentinel. Palo Alto Networks reported in October 2024 that its clients have begun consolidating security across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

Managing security in a multi-cloud environment poses several challenges such as fragmentation of visibility, lack of policy consistency, and increased operational burden. According to AWS, in January 2025, users tend to leverage more than one native or third-party solution, creating further complexity in integration. Cisco also emphasized, in June 2024, that security professionals face difficulties owing to siloed information, resulting in delays in threat response and mitigation.

The use of AI technology in security helps detect threats more quickly, prioritize risks and make remediation easier. CrowdStrike mentions that, in March 2025, AI-driven threat detection for cloud threats was greatly reduced by its Falcon platform. The upgrades to Microsoft’s Security Copilot in April 2025 demonstrate the role of generative AI in investigations and the automation of incident response tasks.

The rapid development of cloud technology is creating a shortage of security professionals who can provide cloud-native security, DevSecOps, and AI solutions. For instance, Fortinet, in its 2024 cybersecurity skills gap report, noted a challenge in recruiting cloud security professionals. Similarly, in November 2024, ISC2 revealed that the teams were working under heavier workloads due to the skills shortage and were, therefore, resorting to automation.

MARKET SEGMENTS

The platforms/solutions segment holds a leading position because companies prefer integrated solutions over separate tools. According to Zscaler, by 2025, companies were expected to adopt a zero-trust architecture and move away from the traditional VPN-based approach. In 2024, Microsoft reported that the adoption of the Defender for Cloud tool was increasing due to its native and AI-based threat detection capabilities.

CNAPP adoption is growing as companies look for end-to-end protection from code development through runtime execution on a single platform. As stated by SentinelOne, in January 2026, major vendors such as Microsoft, Palo Alto Networks, and Wiz are enhancing CNAPP features with agentless visibility and automated compliance. In 2025, Wiz highlighted that clients have shown preference for a single platform that minimizes complexity and prioritizes risks in multi-cloud infrastructures.

The private cloud deployment mode is important in regulated industries that require greater control over critical information. In March 2026, Cisco indicated that companies still run their operations using hybrid clouds, which have both public and private clouds, thus the need to enforce policies uniformly. According to AWS, in 2025, customers started incorporating private cloud controls with public cloud services.

Large organizations are early adopters because of their complex IT infrastructure and heightened risk exposure. Palo Alto Networks claims that by October 2025, enterprise businesses had integrated several security applications into single applications such as Prisma Cloud. In 2024, Zscaler stated that its large-scale customers are fueling its success with an emphasis on secure access and zero-trust solutions.

BFSI emerges as the largest adopter due to the high sensitivity of financial information and stringent regulations. Zscaler reported that by June 2025 financial organizations were expected to leverage AI technologies for threat detection and secure access to counter identity-based attacks. According to Microsoft, in 2024, banks are moving towards cloud-native cybersecurity tools to ensure compliance and safeguard digital payments ecosystems.

REGION

Asia Pacific to be fastest-growing region in global cloud security market during forecast period.

Asia Pacific is likely to be the fastest-growing region in the cloud security market owing to the widespread adoption of cloud computing, digitalization, and cybersecurity regulations in nations such as India, China, and Southeast Asia. According to Amazon Web Services, demand for cloud security services was expected to increase by October 2025 due to the expansion of regional data centers and enterprises migrating to the cloud. In 2024, Microsoft reported that companies in the Asia Pacific region are rapidly adopting security solutions based on artificial intelligence.

AUTHOR DETAILS

Secondary research was conducted to collect information useful for this technical, market-oriented, and commercial study of the cloud security market. The next step involved validating these findings, assumptions, and sizing with industry experts across the value chain using primary research. Different approaches, including top-down and bottom-up methods, were employed to estimate the total market size. After that, the market breakup and data triangulation procedures were used to estimate the market size of the segments and subsegments of the cloud security market.

Secondary Research

During the secondary research process, various secondary sources were consulted to identify and collect information relevant to the study. The secondary sources included annual reports, press releases, investor presentations of cloud security vendors, forums, certified publications, and whitepapers. The secondary research was mainly used to obtain key information about the industry’s supply chain, the total pool of key players, market classification and segmentation according to industry trends to the bottom-most level, regional markets, and key developments from both market- and technology-oriented perspectives, all of which were further validated by primary sources.

Primary Research

In the primary research process, various primary sources from both the supply and demand sides were interviewed to obtain qualitative and quantitative information for this report. The primary sources from the supply side included industry experts such as chief executive officers (CEOs), vice presidents (VPs), marketing directors, technology and innovation directors, and other key executives from major companies and organizations operating in the cloud security market.

In the market engineering process, top-down and bottom-up approaches were extensively used, along with several data triangulation methods, to estimate and forecast the overall market segments and subsegments listed in this report. Market Definition

According to MarketsandMarkets, “The cloud security market refers to the segment of the cybersecurity industry that includes platforms/solutions and services designed to secure cloud-based infrastructure and data across public, private, and hybrid environments. As organizations increasingly adopt cloud computing to support digital transformation, the need to protect distributed and dynamic cloud environments from evolving cyber threats has become a critical priority.

The market primarily includes security solutions such as Cloud-Native Application Protection Platforms (CNAPP) and Cloud Access Security Broker (CASB). Cloud security encompasses a broad set of capabilities for securing cloud workloads, managing access to applications and resources, protecting sensitive data, and enabling continuous monitoring and threat detection. These capabilities are designed to address cloud-specific risks, including configuration vulnerabilities, unauthorized access, data exposure, and application-layer threats, arising from the shared responsibility model and the highly dynamic nature of cloud environments. Security is increasingly embedded across the cloud lifecycle, from development and deployment to runtime operations.

In addition to technology solutions, the market includes services that support the design, implementation, and ongoing management of cloud security environments. Organizations are leveraging external expertise to integrate security into cloud architectures, ensure regulatory compliance and maintain continuous visibility and control across multi-cloud environments. These services play a key role in addressing skill gaps and operational complexities associated with cloud security.
